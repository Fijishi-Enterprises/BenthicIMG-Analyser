Basics

This is a point-count image annotation tool.

The image shows the locations of the points. The points are colored depending on their status: unannotated, machine-annotated, human-annotated, or selected.

The text field list on the right show the current labels for each point.

  • If the label is in black text, the point is human-annotated. Once all points are human-annotated and saved, you'll see an "ALL DONE" indicator below the list, indicating that you are done annotating the image.
  • If the label is in gray text, the point is machine-annotated. Machine labels are just there to give you guidance; you need to confirm these labels (thus making them human-annotated) before they are counted in your source's statistics.

Selecting a point to label

  • Click on one of the text fields on the right, or Shift-click on the image to select a point.
  • Press the . key to go to the first point needing annotation.
  • Move to the next or previous point with Tab and Shift+Tab.

Labeling the chosen point

Once you have chosen a point, a text field will appear over the image. Type a label code in this text field and hit EnterReturn to confirm it.

If you have machine annotations enabled in your Settings and your source, you may see a list of ranked machine suggestions along with probability scores (for example, "Favia [25%]") before you even start typing. From here, you can either pick a suggestion or just start typing your own label. Note that if you want to pick the first suggestion (the same as the light gray text), all you have to do is hit EnterReturn .

Alternatively, below the image is a grid of label buttons; there's one button for each label in your Source's labelset. You can label the current point by clicking the corresponding button.

Saving your work

To save your work, click the "Save Progress" button on the lower right, or hit Ctrl+S Cmd+S. There are two cases where you won't be able to save:

  • When no changes have been made. In this case, the button text will say "Saved".
  • In certain circumstances, a point may somehow get labeled with an invalid label code. In this case, the button text will say "Error".

Selecting multiple points

  • Select multiple points by Ctrl-clicking Cmd-clicking the points on the image.
  • You may also click the numbers next to the text field list.
  • For some quick select options, use these buttons on the upper right: , , and . Hover your mouse over them to see what they do.
  • To use the rectangle select tool, click the rectangle select icon, then click and drag on the image to draw a rectangle. The points contained in that rectangle will be added to your current selection.
  • Once multiple points are selected, you can assign a label to them in the same ways as before: by typing in the text field, picking a suggestion, or clicking a label button.
  • Note: Selecting multiple points will give you more accurate machine suggestions!

Getting a better look at a point

  • To zoom in, left-click on the image or press Shift+↑.
  • To zoom out, right-clickCtrl-click on the image or press Shift+↓.
  • You can also zoom with these buttons on the upper right: , , and .
  • To toggle between different point display modes, use these buttons on the upper right: , , and . Hover your mouse over them to see what they do.
  • If the text field on the image is getting in the way, you can move it to a different location by pressing Shift+→. Press these keys again to move it back.
  • There are two ways to adjust the Brightness and Contrast values: use the sliders, or type a value in the text field. Click "Apply" to apply the new values. Click "Reset" to reset both to 0.
  • There are various Settings (see below) that can make it easier for you to find points, and see what's under a point. You can customize point colors, point marker shapes, and the sizes of point markers and numbers.

Navigate to another image

The bottom-right part of the annotation tool has Prev and Next buttons. These buttons take you to the previous or next image in the image set. Images are ordered by filename.

If you got here from the Browse Images page, the image set is determined by your last image search. Otherwise, the image set consists of all images in the source.

The "Return to Browse" link takes you back to the Browse page for the current image set.

Tip: If you want to go through unclassified or unconfirmed images only, use the Browse search form's "Annotation status" field to narrow down the image set.

Settings

  • To open the annotation tool settings, click the "Settings" button at the top.
  • Remember to Save any settings that you want to keep.
